METROPOLITAN TRANSIT AUTHORITY
PROCUREMENT POLICY AND PROCEDURES MANUAL
Revision 12 — Effective January 1, 2026

CHAPTER 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS

1.1 Purpose
This manual establishes the policies and procedures governing the procurement of goods, services, and construction by the Metropolitan Transit Authority (MTA). All procurement activities shall be conducted in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local laws, including the Federal Transit Administration (FTA) Circular 4220.1F.

1.2 Authority
The Chief Procurement Officer (CPO) has the authority to approve purchases up to $500,000. Purchases exceeding $500,000 require Board of Directors approval. Emergency procurements up to $250,000 may be authorized by the Executive Director.

1.3 Ethics
All procurement personnel must adhere to the MTA Code of Ethics. No employee shall participate in any procurement where they have a financial interest or personal conflict. Gifts from vendors exceeding $25 in value must be reported and declined.

CHAPTER 2: PROCUREMENT METHODS

2.1 Micro-Purchases (Under $10,000)
Purchases under $10,000 may be made without competitive bidding, provided that prices are reasonable and the purchase is distributed equitably among qualified suppliers.

2.2 Small Purchases ($10,000 - $150,000)
Purchases between $10,000 and $150,000 require a minimum of three (3) written quotes from qualified vendors. Award shall be made to the lowest responsive, responsible bidder.

2.3 Sealed Bidding (Over $150,000 for goods/construction)
Formal sealed bids are required for purchases exceeding $150,000 when specifications can be clearly defined. The process includes:
a) Public advertisement for a minimum of 21 calendar days
b) Public bid opening at the designated time and place
c) Award to the lowest responsive, responsible bidder
d) Board approval for contracts exceeding $500,000

2.4 Competitive Proposals (Over $150,000 for services)
When sealed bidding is not practical, competitive proposals may be used. This method allows evaluation based on factors other than price, including:
a) Technical approach and methodology
b) Qualifications and experience
c) Management plan
d) Cost/price analysis

2.5 Sole Source Procurement
Sole source procurement is permitted only when:
a) The item or service is available from only one source
b) A public emergency exists requiring immediate procurement
c) FTA or the awarding agency authorizes noncompetitive proposals
d) After solicitation, competition is determined inadequate
All sole source procurements exceeding $10,000 require written justification and CPO approval.

CHAPTER 3: DISADVANTAGED BUSINESS ENTERPRISE (DBE) PROGRAM

3.1 Policy Statement
The MTA is committed to ensuring nondiscrimination in the award and administration of federally assisted contracts. The MTA's overall DBE goal is 18% of the total value of FTA-assisted contracts.

3.2 Good Faith Efforts
Prime contractors who cannot meet the DBE goal must demonstrate good faith efforts, including:
a) Attending pre-bid meetings where DBE firms are identified
b) Advertising in general circulation and minority-focused media
c) Written notification to DBE firms of subcontracting opportunities
d) Following up with interested DBE firms
e) Providing assistance with bonding, insurance, or financing
f) Using the services of available minority community organizations

3.3 Counting DBE Participation
- Only the work actually performed by the DBE firm counts toward the goal
- The entire amount of the DBE's contract counts if the DBE performs the work with its own forces
- For materials and supplies, only the fee or commission charged counts
- Trucking: 100% of fees for trucking using the DBE's own equipment

CHAPTER 4: CONTRACT ADMINISTRATION

4.1 Contract Types
The MTA utilizes the following contract types:
a) Firm Fixed-Price: Used when the scope is well-defined
b) Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee: Used when the scope cannot be precisely defined
c) Time and Materials: Used for small, ill-defined requirements
d) Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ): Used for recurring needs

4.2 Change Orders
Change orders must be processed for any modification to the original contract scope, schedule, or price. Change orders exceeding 10% of the original contract value require Board approval.

4.3 Payment Processing
a) Invoices must be submitted within 30 days of service delivery
b) The MTA shall process payment within 30 days of invoice approval (Net 30)
c) Prompt payment to subcontractors: Prime contractors must pay subcontractors within 15 days of receiving payment from the MTA
d) Retainage of 5% shall be held on construction contracts until substantial completion

4.4 Contract Closeout
Upon completion of all deliverables, the Project Manager shall initiate the contract closeout process, which includes:
a) Final inspection and acceptance
b) Resolution of all claims and disputes
c) Final payment processing
d) Return of all retention amounts
e) Filing of all required documentation
f) Audit of contract performance and expenditures
