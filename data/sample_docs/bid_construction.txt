INVITATION FOR BID (IFB) NO. 2026-PW-0117
COUNTY OF RIVERSIDE — MAIN STREET BRIDGE REHABILITATION PROJECT

ISSUED BY: Riverside County Department of Public Works
DATE ISSUED: February 3, 2026
BID OPENING: March 15, 2026 at 10:00 AM PST
PRE-BID CONFERENCE: February 20, 2026 at 2:00 PM PST (Mandatory)

ARTICLE I — PROJECT DESCRIPTION

1.1 Riverside County invites sealed bids from qualified contractors for the rehabilitation of the Main Street Bridge (Structure No. RV-2847) spanning the Cedar River.

1.2 The project includes:
- Complete deck replacement (approximately 12,000 square feet)
- Structural steel repair and recoating
- Seismic retrofit of substructure elements
- New bridge railings compliant with MASH TL-4 standards
- Approach roadway reconstruction (200 feet each side)
- Temporary traffic management during construction
- Environmental protection measures per NPDES permit

1.3 Engineer's Estimate: $4,500,000 to $5,200,000

ARTICLE II — BIDDER QUALIFICATIONS

2.1 Bidders must hold a valid Class A General Engineering Contractor license issued by the State Contractors License Board.

2.2 Bidders must demonstrate completion of at least three (3) bridge rehabilitation projects of similar scope (value exceeding $3,000,000) within the past seven (7) years.

2.3 Bidders must have a current Experience Modification Rate (EMR) of 1.0 or lower.

2.4 Bidders must be registered with the Department of Industrial Relations (DIR) as a public works contractor.

ARTICLE III — BID SUBMISSION

3.1 Bids must be submitted on the official Bid Form (Attachment B) provided by the County.

3.2 Each bid must be accompanied by:
a) Bid Security in the amount of 10% of the total bid, in the form of a bid bond, certified check, or cashier's check
b) List of Subcontractors (per the Subletting and Subcontracting Fair Practices Act)
c) Non-Collusion Affidavit
d) DBE Participation Commitment Form
e) Bidder's Statement of Qualifications

3.3 Bids must be sealed and clearly marked "BID — Main Street Bridge Rehabilitation, IFB 2026-PW-0117"

ARTICLE IV — DISADVANTAGED BUSINESS ENTERPRISE (DBE)

4.1 This project has a DBE participation goal of 15% as required by federal funding regulations.

4.2 The County is committed to ensuring equal opportunity for Disadvantaged Business Enterprises in all federally funded projects.

4.3 Bidders who cannot meet the DBE goal must submit documentation of good faith efforts in accordance with 49 CFR Part 26.

4.4 Failure to meet the DBE goal or demonstrate good faith efforts shall render the bid non-responsive.

ARTICLE V — PREVAILING WAGES

5.1 This is a prevailing wage project. All workers employed on this project shall be paid not less than the prevailing rate of per diem wages as determined by the Director of Industrial Relations.

5.2 The contractor shall comply with all provisions of the California Labor Code relating to public works, including but not limited to Sections 1770-1781.

5.3 Certified payroll records must be submitted weekly to the County and electronically to the DIR.

ARTICLE VI — CONTRACT DURATION

6.1 The work shall be completed within 240 calendar days from the date of the Notice to Proceed.

6.2 Liquidated damages of $3,500 per calendar day shall apply for each day the project remains incomplete beyond the contract duration.

6.3 The contractor may request time extensions for delays caused by force majeure events, subject to County approval.

ARTICLE VII — INSURANCE AND BONDING

7.1 Insurance Requirements:
- Commercial General Liability: $5,000,000 per occurrence
- Automobile Liability: $2,000,000 combined single limit
- Workers Compensation: Statutory limits
- Professional Liability: $2,000,000 per claim
- Pollution Liability: $2,000,000 per occurrence
- Builder's Risk: Full contract value

7.2 Bonding Requirements:
- Performance Bond: 100% of contract value
- Payment Bond: 100% of contract value
- Bonds must be issued by a surety company authorized to do business in the State

ARTICLE VIII — PAYMENT TERMS

8.1 Progress payments shall be made monthly based on the Schedule of Values and certified work completed.

8.2 The County shall retain 5% of each progress payment until the project reaches 50% completion, after which retention may be reduced to 2.5% at the County's discretion.

8.3 Final payment shall be made within 60 days after formal acceptance of the completed work by the County.

8.4 Payment terms: Net 30 days from receipt of approved invoice and certified payroll records.
